I started in print, designing album covers, promo, and apparel, shaping visuals that carried identity and mood. That path led me into the web during the Flash era, when design, motion, and code began to blur together. I explored interaction, animation, and front-end work, always grounded in visual thinking and how things feel in motion.

Over time, the work shifted. It became less about individual pieces and more about how everything connects. I moved into product design, focusing on structure, systems, and experiences that need to scale and hold together. Today, I bring it all together, using generative tools and AI-assisted coding to design and build products, creative systems, and digital experiences.

With over a decade of experience across product design, visual systems, and creative technology, I've worked on consumer and enterprise products at companies like Disney, Life Force Academy, Citrix, and PwC. I started in print, motion, and front-end, which still shapes how I approach design today. I'm Venezuelan-born, educated in the South, with roots in South Florida and now based in the Bay Area. These experiences have shaped how I see people, culture, and the systems I design for. More recently, my work has expanded into a creative technologist space, using generative tools and AI-assisted coding to prototype and build faster, bringing together visual thinking, structure, and execution across product and creative work. Outside of work, I enjoy being in nature — hiking, time in the woods, and the quiet calm of Florida's beaches.
